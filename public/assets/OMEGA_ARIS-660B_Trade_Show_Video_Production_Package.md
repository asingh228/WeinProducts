## 1. Creative Strategy

### The Big Idea: "The Coin You Can't See"
Every person walking past the booth owns or trades coins they have never seen the inside of. The video weaponizes that single, universal anxiety. We don't sell a machine for the first 40 seconds — we sell *doubt*, then we resolve it with the ARIS-660B as the only instrument that interrogates the full internal volume of the coin, not just its skin.

### Why this works on a trade show floor
- **Silent-first design.** Every beat lands through bold kinetic typography and visual metaphor. Audio (VO + sound design) is a bonus layer for booth visitors standing close, never a requirement.
- **The 8-second hook rule.** Foot traffic decides in under 8 seconds whether to stop. We open with a beautiful coin being digitally "sliced" to reveal a grey tungsten core — an image that is impossible to walk past if you own gold.
- **Loop-engineered.** The final frame visually rhymes with the opening frame, so the video loops seamlessly and a viewer can enter at any point and still get hooked at the next cycle.
- **Problem → Physics → Proof → CTA.** Dealers are skeptical and technically literate. We give them real physics (skin effect, thermal drift, volumetric coverage) in visual form — credibility is the conversion mechanism with this audience.

### Compliance note (per client claim-control guidelines)
All on-screen copy below uses the client's approved defensive phrasing:
- "Full-volume acoustic analysis" (not "100% volumetric map")
- "Verifies the internal structure in seconds" (not "in 3 seconds flat")
- "Surface-oriented electromagnetic checks can leave internal risks unresolved" (not absolute claims about competitors)
- The closing card carries the client's multi-method disclaimer.

### Visual System
- **Palette:** Dark slate / gunmetal backgrounds (#1A1F26), authentic gold (#D4A437) and silver highlights, crimson warning accents (#D7263D) for fraud, emerald (#19B26B) for verification.
- **Typography:** Ultra-bold condensed sans (e.g., Druk, Tungsten — fittingly — or Archivo Black) in white/gold; kinetic, full-screen, high contrast. Minimum on-screen text size: legible at 6 meters.
- **Motion language:** Slow, luxurious coin macro shots for "trust"; hard cuts, glitch frames, and crimson scan lines for "threat"; smooth concentric wave animations for "solution."

## 5. Production Notes

### Silent-playback integrity checklist
Every shot must pass this test: *muted, viewed from 6 meters, mid-loop entry* — does the viewer still understand the beat? The typography above is written to carry 100% of the narrative on its own. Do not shrink type to fit more words; cut words instead.

- **The "Read-It-Twice" pacing rule:** When timing the kinetic typography in post, hold each text card long enough that a distracted, walking viewer could read it **twice** before the cut. If a line can't survive that hold without dragging the shot, shorten the line — not the hold. (Rule of thumb: ~0.5 sec per word, minimum 2.5 sec per card.)
- **Hardcoded minimalist subtitles:** Burn in clean, small, bottom-center subtitles for the VO track on the master. The kinetic typography carries the headline narrative; the subtitles carry the full technical detail for viewers who want it over the convention-hall noise. Keep them visually quiet (white, 60–70% opacity, thin weight) so they never compete with the hero typography. Maintain a safe gap between subtitle zone and kinetic text zone — never let them overlap.

### Loop engineering
- Shot 11 ends on the same coin/rotation state Shot 1 begins with. Match the rotation angle and lighting in the edit so the cut is invisible.
- Audio bed should resolve to the same ambient hum it opens with.

### AI video generation workflow (recommended)
0. **Prototype caution:** The current product photo is a prototype; hardware revisions are expected from the client. Use the prototype image only to **pipeline-test** model fidelity (run the Shot 7 prompt through Runway and Kling, compare subject preservation, pick the winner). Generate **final** takes of all device shots (7–11) only from photos of the revised unit. Shots 1–6 contain no device and can go to final generation immediately.
1. **Inputs to gather from client:** (a) 3–5 high-res product photos of the ARIS-660B from front, 3/4, and top angles on neutral background; (b) high-res photos of a gold and a silver coin (obverse, reverse, edge); (c) the existing cutaway diagram (already supplied — use as structural reference for Shots 2, 5, 8). **Photo quality note:** the current real-unit photo is shot on a green backdrop with visible cables — usable for geometry/mechanism reference, but reshoot (or cleanly composite) on a dark neutral background with cables dressed before feeding it as the AI base image, or the models will reproduce the green spill and cable clutter.
2. **Model guidance:** Use an image-to-video model with strong subject preservation (Runway Gen-3/Gen-4, Kling 1.6+, or Sora image-conditioning) for all product shots (7–11) — hardware fidelity is non-negotiable for a client demo. Pure text-to-video is acceptable only for Shots 3 and 6.
3. **Generate at 16:9, 4K or upscale**; each prompt above targets a 6–10 second clip — generate 2–3 takes per shot and edit to the timecodes.
4. **Typography is added in post** (After Effects / Premiere), never inside the AI generation — AI-generated text is unreliable and off-brand. The prompts deliberately reserve negative space for overlays.
5. **Consistency pass:** AI clips generated across different tools (or even different runs of the same tool) will drift in color. Once all clips are on the timeline, apply **one unified grade across the entire master**: push shadows into the dark slate/gunmetal (#1A1F26) and warm the highlights toward the brand gold (#D4A437).
